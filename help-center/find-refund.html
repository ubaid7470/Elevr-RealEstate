    Find your refund amount

    Typically, the refund you receive is determined by your reservation's cancellation policy and the timing of your cancellation. To ascertain the refund amount and the specific cancellation policy applicable to your reservation, you can initiate the cancellation process for your stay. Another way to access the cancellation policy for your reservation is by navigating to Trips, selecting your trip, or referencing your reservation confirmation email. Both avenues provide details about the cancellation policy associated with your booking.

    More info

    • Partial payment: If you've only made a partial upfront payment for your stay, your refund may be less than anticipated and will never surpass the amount you paid initially.
    • Refund timing: While we process refunds promptly upon cancellation, it's probable that your refund won't arrive immediately.
    • Cancellation cutoff times: The cutoff times for cancellations adhere to the trip's local time.
    • Coupons: Please note, coupons are non-refundable.
    • Service fees: Service fees associated with stays are non-refundable if you cancel after the free refund period specified in your reservation.

    Special Cases

    You might be eligible for either a full refund or a refund larger than the standard amount stipulated in your Host's cancellation policy if:

    • You're canceling due to anextenuating circumstance.
    • Your cancellation aligns with a qualifying reason outlined in the Evelr Rebooking and Refund Policy.
    • While staying, you encounter an issue covered by the Evelr Rebooking and Refund Policy.

    COVID-19

    If your plans have shifted due to COVID-19, verify whether the Extenuating Circumstances Policy is applicable to your reservation. Refunds for canceled stays due to COVID-19 necessitate evidence of illness or exposure within 14 days before check-in. This proof may include:

    • A positive COVID-19 test result
    • A doctor's note
    • Notification from local government confirming exposure
    • A screenshot of a notification from a contact tracing application
    • Positive test results from a household member
