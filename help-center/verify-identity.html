    Verify your identity

    Our goal is to build trust between guests and hosts on Evelr. While no identification process is foolproof, we strive to instill confidence in the authenticity of our users. Hence, we have verification procedures in place. We mandate verification for listing hosts, new Co-Hosts, and booking guests. When you book or become a host, we might verify your legal name, address, phone number, and other details. A government ID photo and a selfie may also be requested. Some Experiences require identity verification. For more information on our trust and safety measures, please refer to our Privacy Policy and explore our commitment to trust and security at Evelr.

    Why we verify identify

    Confirming the identity of guests and hosts aids us in:

    • Detecting deceptive conduct
    • Conducting background verifications where allowable by relevant laws
    • Assessing potential unauthorized access to an account
    • Exploring safety concerns like assault, theft, or illicit actions

    Why we verify identify

    As part of the identity verification process, we may ask you to provide us with:

    • Legal name, address, or other personal details: Sometimes, these details alone are enough for us to authenticate your identity.
    • Government ID photo: This may include a driver's license, passport, or identity card.
    • Selfie: At times, we might request a photo with your government ID. If providing a matching selfie is challenging, feel free to reach out to us for alternative verification options.

    How long does it takes to verify ID

    Once you've submitted your information, it may take up to 24 hours for the ID verification to be reviewed and approved.

    Verifying your identity in your account settings

    You can verify your identity in your account settings by submitting a photo of your government ID. You can do this when setting up an account – or when updating your account details.

    Where we display your identity verification status

    Your identity verification status may be indicated on your listing with the label “identity verified” or across Evelr as an orange badge with a checkmark next to your profile photo. Your identity verification status is also shown in your Profile in the section titled [Your name]'s confirmed information.

    Having Trouble?

    • Check Messages: Look for info via text, email, or phone. Need camera access? Refresh Evelr if it's not working.
    • Photo Quality: Take well-lit, clear photos fitting the white lines. Use "auto" mode for better pics.
    • ID Guidelines: Send photos of original, undamaged IDs, both sides for two-sided IDs.
    • Valid IDs: No school, college, library, gym, or military IDs. Explore accepted IDs on Evelr.

    When we verify your identity

    Once you've submitted your information, it may take up to 24 hours for the ID verification to be reviewed and approved.As a guest, you'll need to verify your identity before booking, and as a host, when creating your first listing. Hosts offering accommodations who haven't completed ID verification might not receive new bookings until they do so.

    Booking

    Guests booking stays or Experiences need to verify their identity during checkout, usually taking under a minute. It speeds up confirmation and helps ensure a seamless booking process. If needed, we may ask for additional details or a government ID photo to verify your identity swiftly.

    Hosting

    For new hosts creating listings or Experiences, completing identity verification using government ID or other details is necessary before publishing. Co-Hosts invited to join must verify their identity to accept. Existing hosts, Co-Hosts listed as Primary, or Experience hosts should complete verification promptly. Failure may lead to consequences, like calendar blocking. If your calendar is blocked due to pending verification, new reservations won't be possible until this step is fulfilled.

    What a Host may ask you for

    The host might request ID during in-person check-in if it's in the house rules or required by local laws at booking. Certain areas mandate guest registration with local authorities. This could happen directly or through accommodation on Airbnb's platform, including various types of stays.
